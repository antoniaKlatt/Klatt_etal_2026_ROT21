# Natural and human-driven changes of phytoplankton community composition in Rotsee (Switzerland) since the Younger Dryas 
## Antonia Klatt, Fatemeh Ajallooeian, Petra Zahajská, Ricardo N. Santos, Daniel B. Nelson, Sebastian Naeher, Carsten J. Schubert, Nathalie Dubois, Cindy De Jonge & S. Nemiah Ladd
[Link]

In this study, we analyzed a 13 kyr sediment record from Swiss lake Rotsee (ROT21) to assess the impact of climate variability and eutrophication on the relative abundance of cyanobacteria. We reconstructed the relative proportions of cyanobacteria and eukaryotic algae based on the abundance of phytol and phytosterols and compound-specific hydrogen isotope ratios of C16:0 fatty acid and phytol. We combined these lipid-based proxies with hyperspectral imaging (HSI) scans of phycocyanin and pigment distributions from pigment extracts. We further used HSI scans of bacteriopheophytin *a* and *e* as proxies for lake stratification. Additionally, we compared the distributions of phytol and phytosterols in ROT21 with published high-resolution data from a previously collected short core (ROT09) from Naeher et al. (2012) (https://doi.org/10.1016/j.orggeochem.2012.05.014) to assess the impact of 20th century eutrophication on the phytoplankton community.

#### This repository contains all ROT21 datasets from this study and respective R scripts for analysis and visualization. 

### 01_Data

This folder contains datasets from ROT21 as well as ROT09 used in this study.

**NOTE**: 
All lipid data from ROT09 are from from Naeher et al. (2012) (https://doi.org/10.1016/j.orggeochem.2012.05.014); 
ROT21 δ2H C25 *n*-alkane data are from Santos et al. (2026) (https://doi.org/10.1029/2025PA005401)

General note: ROT21 sample ages are based on the published age-depth model from De Jonge et al. (2025) (https://doi.org/10.1007/s10933-025-00361-3)

### 02_R_scripts

This folder contains R codes for data analysis and plotting.
